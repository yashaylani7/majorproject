Introduction

Autism, or autism spectrum disorder (ASD), is a complex condition that affects social skills, behavior, communication, and daily tasks. Children with autism often learn differently from other children, and it is important to consider their unique characteristics when developing learning tools for them. In recent years, technology such as computers and smartphones has been found to be engaging for autistic children.

Many resources and applications have been developed to support the learning of autistic children, but they often do not take into account the cultural and local knowledge of children in countries like Bangladesh. For instance, a software module that teaches about different types of fruit may include apples and strawberries for children in first-world countries, but for autistic children in Bangladesh, it would be more relevant to include mangoes and jackfruits.

It is important to continue researching and developing resources and tools that are suitable for autistic children in a variety of cultural and local contexts. By doing so, we can ensure that all children with autism have the opportunity to learn and grow to their full potential.

Purpose

This document is a concise explanation of the Software requirements and specifications of EmPower. It is the bible that contains all the requirements and documentation. It lays the framework that every team involved in development will follow. It's used to provide critical information to multiple teams — development, quality assurance, operations, and maintenance ensuring the teams agree. This document defines what is needed from the product. It states the product's purpose and what it must achieve. All the functionalities, objects, and developing processes are explicitly described in this document which will later be helpful for the project verification and testing.

Intended Audience

To create a versatile, efficient, and convenient system, developers must fully comprehend its future functions, users, and supposed benefits. Only after this step, they will be able to plan and coordinate their efforts, choose the most appropriate technologies, anticipate potential problems, and come up with solutions. Empower, as its name suggests, is a complex software suite of interconnected program modules that serve to provide both information and management capabilities to a large variety of users. The supposed users of Empower may be generally divided into three categories:

  1.  Instructors (Teachers)
  2.  Students
  3. Guardians (Parents)

Each category should have a separate set of rules for clearance and restrictions within the system. This set depends on a user’s role and varies from unrestricted access granted to the Instructors to limited access to a certain portion of information for students and guardians. For example, a teacher should have access to his students’ data, but a student should not be allowed to get information on teachers’ data. Every user should have a unique identifier, which associates the person with that person’s rank in the system. Users with higher ranks and more permissions within the system may need additional means of authorization.

Conclusion

This software requirement specification is more than helpful to identify the owner and stakeholders of the project. As it is working as the baseline of the project which reflects the demand of the school managing party, instructors, guardians, and students so that the system is up to the function it needs to. As this document is addressed as the bible of the project, the document content may evolve with the passage of modification and enhancement of the software whenever it is necessary.

Chapter 1: Inception Report of EmPower

1.1 Introduction

The development of intelligent devices and applications in technology has produced several practical methods for dealing with ASD quickly and effectively. Even though everyone should have a basic education, traditionally teaching the autistic community is still rather difficult. The interaction challenges - such as how autistic children may interact with the learning software or devices - affect ICT-based learning software that has been created for autistic children. Our tool EmPower is intended to solve these issues. This particular system is based on the SAND Autism School, Gazipur which is a self-governing institution, posing unique challenges for our work. This document will serve as a stepping stone for further activities regarding the project and will contain the identities and necessities of stakeholders.

Successful completion of a project depends on the correct understanding of the project, which depends on two things.

  1. Strong understanding, bonding, and mutual respect between the team members.
  2. Overcoming the communication barrier between the client and the team.

To develop the understanding between team members, we arranged multiple team meetings both online and offline which helped us to overcome the initial hesitation. Chapter 2: Elicitation of EmPower

Elicitation is the process to determine the requirements for an intended software system by communicating with clients, end users, system users, and others who have a stake in the software system development. Before requirements can be analyzed, modeled, or specified, they must be gathered. Gathering requirements is part of the requirements engineering process and is usually followed by requirements analysis and specification.

Commonly used research methods are stakeholder meetings or interviews. For example, an essential first meeting takes place between the software engineer and the customer, where they can discuss their views on the requirements.

2.1 Quality Function Deployment

Quality Function Deployment (QFD) is a structured approach to defining customer

needs or requirements and translating them into specific plans to produce products

to meet those needs. The “voice of the customer” is the term to describe these

stated and unstated customer needs or requirements.

We visited SAND Autism School and talked to the authority, teachers, guardians, and other employees and got the following requirements for our automated system:

2.1.1 Normal Requirements:

The objectives and goals are stated for a product or system during meetings with the customer. If these requirements are present, the customer is satisfied. Examples of standard conditions might be requested types of graphical displays, specific system functions, and defined levels of performance. The Normal conditions we came up with are the following:

  • We have to teach four kinds of things through this learning tool. These are as follows:
  • Nouns, i.e. identifying objects - We have to teach nouns through images, texts, and audio.
  • Verbs i.e. identifying tasks - Verbs are the same as nouns i.e. teach with images, texts, and audio.
  • Activity Scheduling i.e. learning to do work sequentially - Activity scheduling needs to be taught with videos and texts.
  • Association i.e. understanding relations between correlated things - Association may contain images, texts, and audio or videos and texts.
  • The instructors can create customized lessons.
  • To evaluate the learning outcomes, there should be an evaluation system. The evaluation system consists of three options. Such as -
  • MCQ - There will be an image and a question regarding the image and answer options.
  • Drag & Drop - This is an image drag & drop matching problem. Users have to drag images and drop them in the correct section. For example, matching a picture of a dog to the word "dog" or matching the phrase "I am happy" to an emoticon with a smiling face.
  • Sequence Exercise - These exercises involve arranging a set of items (e.g., pictures, words, or phrases) in a specific order. For example, arranging a series of pictures to create a story or arranging words to form a sentence.
  • Jigsaw Puzzle - An image will be divided into several parts and these will be shuffled. Users match the image parts by dragging & dropping. Puzzles will contain different levels that can be useful for different ASD children.
  • All the instructions will be available in Bangla.

2.2 Usage Scenario of EmPower

The term "autism" or "autism spectrum disorder" (ASD) refers to a wide variety of developmental problems that affect behavior, speech, and social interaction. The development of intelligent devices and applications in technology has produced several practical methods for dealing with this sickness quickly and effectively. Even though everyone should have a primary education, traditionally teaching the autistic community is still rather difficult. The interaction challenges such as how autistic children may interact with the learning software or devices, affect ICT-based learning software that has been created for autistic children.

2.2.1 EmPower - A Learning Tool:

EmPower is a user-friendly interactive learning tool to provide autistic children with basic academics. To teach the students, EmPower provides different lessons on nouns, verbs, activities, and associations that are made concerning different types of disabilities that necessitate different requirements. There is an interactive evaluation system and a system to monitor and track the learning progress of students based on their gaze tracking system.

Empower is a desktop application. It comes in two different versions, one for the instructors and another one for the students. Each of the users has to install their relevant version into their system.

Each instructor has to set a unique username (according to a given defined format) and his/her name into the system. Instructors assign each student a unique student id (according to a given defined format) and save the necessary information about the student (guardian name, guardian phone number) into the system. While recording the performance of students instructors record each student's performance with respect to his/her student id and also put his/her username.

With limited access to all features of our software, students can view the lessons, and solve the assessment queries. On the contrary, instructors have access to all features of our software including adding lessons, customizing lessons, creating assessment questions, tracking, monitoring individual and cumulative students' performance, and analyzing and viewing the overall summary of the student’s performance in the form of different graphical representations.

2.2.2 Lesson Management:

Our learning tool will teach students four skills: identifying objects (nouns) using images, texts, and audio; identifying tasks (verbs) using images, texts, and audio; sequencing tasks (activity scheduling) using videos and texts; and understanding associations between related things using images, texts, audio, and/or videos and texts.

The instructors can make customized lessons according to specific students' needs. They will add images, text, audio, and videos related to specific topics. They can also reorganize the orientation of the contents i.e. they can flexibly place the image, text, audio, or video in any location on the screen. That is necessary as all of the children’s perspectives might not be the same. Therefore instructors can reorder the contents to match individuals' perspectives to interact with the system.

The students can view the lessons. They’ll learn by observing the images/videos in addition to the relevant text and audio to understand the context.

There is a reward system as Autistic children are highly motivated by rewards. Reward refers to a clapping image, a happy emoji, or a cartoon video. The instructor can choose a reward according to the student's taste and the student will be rewarded for completing a task.

2.2.3 Evaluation System:

Our evaluation system for learning outcomes includes three types of evaluations:

MCQ - there will be an image and a question regarding the image and answer options, and Drag & Drop - This is an image drag & drop matching problem. Users have to drag images and drop them in the correct section. For example, matching a picture of a dog to the word "dog" or matching the phrase "I am happy" to an emotion with a smiling face, Sequence Exercise - These exercises involve arranging a set of items (e.g., pictures, words, or phrases) in a specific order. For example, arranging a series of pictures to create a story or arranging words to form a sentence, Jigsaw Puzzle - An image will be divided into several parts and these will be shuffled. Users match the image parts by dragging & dropping. Puzzles will contain different levels that can be useful for different ASD children. Instructors will observe the results and provide necessary feedback and marks to grade their performance.

Instructors can modify the contents of the assessment question. They can set MCQ questions with options, select images for puzzles, set the level of difficulty, and can take screenshots to make the sequencing exercise.

After attempting the assessment tests, the records of each student will be stored in a log file and sent to the instructor for further evaluation. Instructors will grade the individual's performance and the results will be stored in the instructor's system. As each child possesses a different personality, therefore their grading will fluctuate in accordance with it.

2.2.4 Surveillance Management:

While learning the lessons the activities of the students are monitored through the webcam. The software tracks the student’s eyeball (iris) movements, denoting how attentive he/she was while solving the assessment problems or learning the lessons. Here attentiveness is measured based on the duration a student spends on each lesson or assessment problem. All the details will be stored for further analysis. The software provides a statistical analysis based on the collected data and predicts the students' performance.

2.2.5 Progress Monitoring:

2.2.5.1 Data Collection:

Our software automatically saves students’ lessons and task data (assessment performance, and activities) on their system. The guardian of the students collects the data in a pen drive and then provides it to the instructor. The instructor records the data for specific students.

2.2.5.2 Performance evaluation:

Based on students’ performance (lessons, assessment) data, a performance report will be generated by the system. Both the instructor and guardian can view the reports. Instructors will have the data for each student and they can compare the performance among those students through different visualizations. Different graphical elements like graphs and charts will be available to visualize the insights into student performance. So that the instructors can track the student's progress and make necessary adjustments to their teaching methods.

Chapter 3: Scenario-Based Modeling

The success of a computer-based system or product is measured in many ways but user satisfaction resides at the top of the list. Understanding how actors want to interact with a system will lead the software team to properly characterize requirements and build meaningful analysis and design models. Hence, requirements modeling begins with the creation of scenarios in the form of use cases, activity diagrams, and swimlane diagrams.

3.1 What is a Use Case Diagram?

A use case is a written description of how users will perform tasks on your website. It outlines, from a user's point of view, a system's behavior as it responds to a request. Each use case is represented as a sequence of simple steps, beginning with a user's goal and ending when that goal is fulfilled. Use cases specify the expected behavior (what), and not the exact method of making it happen (how). Use cases once specified can be denoted by both textual and visual representation (i.e. use case diagram). A key concept of use case modeling is that it helps us design a system from the end user's perspective. It is an effective technique for communicating system behavior in the user's terms by specifying all externally visible system behavior.

3.2 The Purpose of Use Case Diagram

The reasons why an organization would want to use case diagrams include:

  • Represent the goals of systems and users.
  • Specify the context in which a system should be viewed.
  • Specify system requirements.
  • Provide a model for the flow of events when it comes to user interactions.
  • Provide an outside view of a system.
  • Shows external and internal influences on a system.

3.7 What is a Swimlane Diagram?

The Swimlane diagram, also known as the Rummler-Brache diagram or a cross-functional diagram, is a more detailed form of an activity diagram. Swimlanes are sometimes called functional bands. It simply describes who is responsible for the activities being performed in the activity diagram and how they are responsible. The activity diagram only represents the activities being performed, but Swimlane describes who does what in a process or activity performed.

3.8 The purpose of a Swimlane diagram

A swimlane diagram provides various facilities to a software engineer, such as

  • The separate lanes of the diagram make it easy to delineate responsibilities belonging to certain actors. This helps to clarify complex processes within the software.
  • Visualizing processes in this way provides a more thorough overview of an actor’s roles within an organization and helps to reduce bottlenecks, redundancies, and extraneous steps.
  • Ensures that everyone knows their specific role and avoids collisions.
  • Helps to standardize work processes and record them in highly shareable formats that people within the organization can quickly refer to if needed.

4.1.6 Analysis

Before proceeding to finalize class cards, we need to analyze our list of objects based on their attributes and methods. Depending on their attributes & operations we may need to remove objects and make a superclass / abstract class or interface. And this discussion is now delineated as follows:

  • In the same sense, Audio, Video, and Image also share similar attributes and operations. The actual difference resides mainly in their file type and format. Therefore, it would be beneficial if we make them subclasses of a class, Content where Content is an Abstract class.  .

  • Graphs class contains the properties to visualize the progress report of the students. The Visualization class also shares almost the same properties as Graphs. Therefore, we can merge the Graphs class into the Visualization class.

  • MCQ, Puzzle, Drag & Drop, and Sequencing of them are a type of Evaluation process. Also, both classes share the same properties. Therefore, we changed the Evaluation class to an  abstract superclass and the other two classes to their subclass.

  • Reward class is mainly concerned with images, and sometimes deals with audio also. So, we don’t feel the urge to keep it a single class. Therefore, we’ve merged this class with the Image and Audio classes.

4.2 CRC (Class-Responsibility-Collaboration) Card

Class-responsibility-collaboration (CRC) cards are a brainstorming tool used in the design of object-oriented software. CRC cards are a great tool by which multiple alternative interactions may be quickly devised, as they avoid a great deal of drawing and erasing. CRC card sessions may be followed by the creation of State Transition diagrams to capture interactions that are identified.

CRC cards are frequently employed during the design phase of system and software development to transition use-case descriptions into class diagrams, allowing a smoother transition with a greater overview and permitting developers to implement solutions with low binding and high cohesion.

Chapter 5. Behavioral Modeling: EmPower

The behavioral model indicates how the software will respond to external events or stimuli. In the context of behavioral modeling, two different characterizations of states must be considered: (1) the state of each class as the system performs its function and (2) the state of the system as observed from the outside as the system performs its function.

5.1 State Transition Diagram

One component of a behavioral model is a UML state diagram that represents

active states for each class and the events (triggers) that cause changes between

these active states.  State Transition Diagram represents active states for each class of events (triggers). For this, we identified all the events, their initiators, and collaborators. In the State Transition Diagram, the states are shown in boxed texts, and the transition is represented by arrows. It is also called State Chart or Graph. It is useful in identifying valid transitions.

5.2. Sequence Diagram

The second type of behavioral representation, called a sequence diagram in UML, represents how events cause flow from one object to another as a function of time. The sequence diagram is a shorthand version of the use case. It represents vital classes and the events that cause behavior to flow from class to class.

5.2.1 Purpose of Sequence Diagram

  • High-level interaction between active objects in a system
  • The interaction between object instances within a collaboration that realizes a use case
  • The interaction between objects within a collaboration that realizes an operation
  • Either generic model interactions (showing all possible paths through the

interaction) or specific instances of an interaction (showing just one path through the interaction)

In our project EmPower, we have to show the interactions a user goes through while surfing the whole system from start to finish and organize them according to the segmentation of time. It can be useful to represent the details of UML use cases and see how objects and components interact to complete a process. Moreover, it will help us plan and understand the detailed functionality of the existing scenario we are exploring.
